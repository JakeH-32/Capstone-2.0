PROBLEM STATEMENT:

Recreational SCUBA divers have to plan out how many air tanks they will need in order to safely dive. The amount varies per diver because each person consumes air at a different rate. This rate is commonly called Surface Air Consumption. You want to figure whether you can afford the air requirements for the trips that you are planning.

Write an algorithm that asks for:

    • The total dive time for the trip in minutes 
    • The air budget (in dollars--do not worry about the dollar sign)

 

Your program should then output:

    • The number of liters needed for the project.
    • The estimated cost (in dollars--do not output a dollar sign, do not round to the nearest)
    • Either "Over Budget" or "Within Budget", depending on whether the cost of compressed air is greater than or not greater than the budget, respectfully.

 

Assume that your Surface Air Consumption is 14.5 liters/minute, and that air costs 2 cents per liter. You may assume that all inputs are non-negative numbers.
