PROBLEM STATEMENT:

A 3D printer converts computer generated models into physical objects by heating and extruding a filament known as Polylactic Acid (PLA). PLA, normally sold in spools, is directly fed to the printer head like a thread. You want to determine if you can afford to 3D print a project given its volume of material.

Write an algorithm that asks for:

    • The volume of the 3D model to be printed (in cm3)
    • The user's budget (in dollars--do not worry about the dollar sign, do not round to the nearest)

 

Your program should then output:

    • The length of material (in meters) needed to produce the object
    • The estimated cost (in dollars--do not output a dollar sign)
    • Either "Over Budget" or "Within Budget", depending on whether the cost of materials is greater than or not greater than the user's budget, respectfully.

Assume that each cm3 requires 0.312 meters of PLA to print, and that PLA filament costs $0.063 per meter. You may assume that all inputs are non-negative numbers.
